What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple index of weight-for-height that is commonly used to classify underweight, overweight and obesity in adults. It is defined as a person's weight in kilograms divided by the square of his height in meters (kg/m²).

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 to 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 to 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or greater

These categories are intended to be a general guide and do not apply to athletes, children, pregnant women, or the elderly.

Understanding Calories

A calorie is a unit of energy. In nutrition, calories refer to the energy people get from the food and drink they consume, and the energy they use in physical activity.

Maintaining a healthy weight means balancing the calories you eat and drink with the calories you burn. To lose weight, you must eat fewer calories than your body uses.

Obesity

Causes: Consuming more calories than you burn, sedentary lifestyle, genetics, and certain medications.

Effects: Increased risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, certain cancers, and musculoskeletal disorders.

Prevention: Eating a balanced diet, regular physical activity, and maintaining a healthy weight.

Healthy Lifestyle Tips

  • Eat a balanced diet: Include plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  • Stay active: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic activity a week.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Get enough sleep: Aim for 7 to 9 hours of quality sleep per night.
  • Manage stress: Practice relaxation techniques such as meditation or yoga.
